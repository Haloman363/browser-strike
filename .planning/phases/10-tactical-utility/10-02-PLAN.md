# Phase 10: Tactical Utility - Plan 02: Dynamic Molotov Fire Flow

**Objective:** Overhaul the legacy Molotov effect with a high-fidelity, compute-driven TSL fire simulation that dynamically pools and spreads on surfaces.

## Key Goals
1. **Fire Simulation**: Implement a TSL compute shader in `FXSystem.js` for 500+ fire particles.
2. **Surface Spreading**: Logic for particles to "pool" and expand radially on flat surfaces.
3. **Immersive Visuals**: Multi-stage fire material (core, flame, smoke) using TSL nodes.
4. **Dynamic Damage**: Integrated damage logic based on actual particle positions (radial check).
5. **Legacy Cleanup**: Remove CPU-based `createFireArea` from `main.js`.

## Proposed Changes

### `src/systems/FXSystem.js`
- **Storage Buffer**: `fireStorage` for `struct { position vec3, velocity vec3, age float, life float, spreadFactor float }`.
- **Compute Shader**: `computeFireUpdate` and `computeFireSpawn`.
- **Material**: `fireMaterial` using `SpriteNodeMaterial` with a gradient from white (core) to orange (flame) to black/transparent (smoke).
- **Methods**: `initFire()`, `spawnMolotovFire(position)`.

### `src/systems/PhysicsSystem.js` or `src/systems/RoundSystem.js`
- (Optionally) Add logic to check for players inside active fire areas based on `FXSystem` state.

### `main.js`
- Update `explode` case for `MOLOTOV` to call `fxSystem.spawnMolotovFire(position)`.
- Delete `createFireArea` and its associated logic.

## Verification Plan
1. **Visual Test**: Throw Molotovs and verify the fire "spreads" on the ground and looks realistic.
2. **Performance Test**: Monitor FPS with multiple Molotovs active.
3. **Logic Test**: Stand in the fire and verify damage is taken consistently.
